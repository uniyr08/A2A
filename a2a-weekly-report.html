Reading The Data — What It Tells Us

📉 Why Impressions Went Down — And Why That's OK

Impressions dropped from 31,908 to 17,296 this week. At first glance that looks like a step back. It is not.
PMAX is a smart campaign — as it learns, it stops showing the ad to audiences that are not converting and concentrates on those that are. Fewer impressions + better CTR + a real call = the algorithm is qualifying the audience.
This is normal and healthy PMAX behaviour. The goal is not maximum impressions — it is maximum conversions from the right audience. This week proved that is happening.

📞 Call Conversion — March 23

The campaign recorded one call conversion on Sunday March 23 — the busiest day of the week with 20 clicks. This is logged as a conversion signal in the account.
Intent behind the call is unconfirmed at this stage. We are treating it as an early data point — one that gives the PMAX algorithm a conversion signal to learn from, which is useful regardless of the call outcome.
As conversion data accumulates over the coming weeks, the pattern will become clearer and we will be able to assess lead quality more confidently.
